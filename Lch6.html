CASOR STRUCTURE


The Christ Ambassadors Students' Outreach (CASOR), Nigeria is made up of six zones (South-South, South-East, South-West-North- East, North-West, North-Central) and many sub-zones and chapter. As contained in this spring college training/orientation manual, the vision for this Students' Outreach (CASOR) was conceived in 1971 at the SINKOR CHAPEL in MONROVIA, LIBERIA byJ3io Cyril T. Onyenadum (now a retired Rev. Minister of the Assemblies of God Church).

The first chapter of CASOR was established in 1975 at the University of Nigeria Nsuka. CASOR Rivers State University Port Harcourt Chapter was opened 1984/1985 session with Amb. Edet Asim as the pioneer coordinator while Amb. (now Rev.) Okechukwu Akiri (General Overseer of Oil of Joy Ministry) was the first executive President.

CASOR at its national level has a national executive council made up of three officials - the National President, National Vice President Sister Leader and National General Secretary. She has an appointed National Coordinator courtesy of the Assemblies of God Church, Nigeria to assist him for effective service delivery is the Rational Training Secretary and six other Training Secretaries for six zones.

CASOR Rivers State University belongs to the South-South zone. Each of these six zones mentioned above have zonal executives with only the presidents serving as students' representative at the national level during national meetings. Other than the six main zones, CASOR has many sub-zones (various states in the federation) which are under their respective zones. In the South-South zones, we have six sub-zones which include: Rivers State sub-zone (all the tertiary institutions in Rivers State), Dekta sub-zone, Akwa Ibom sub-zone, Bayelsa sub-zone, Cross Rives sub-zone and Edo sub-zone.
CASOR Rivers State University chapter belongs to the Rivers State sub-zone under the leadership of elected sub-zonal executives with the close supervision of an appointed sub-zonal Training Secretary. Other chapters in the Rivers State sub-zone include: CASOR Uniport chapter, CASOR College of Health and Science Technology (COHSTECH) chapter, CASOR Ken Saro Wiwa Polytechnic (KENPOLY) chapter, CASOR Ignatius Ajuru of Education chapter, (main campus at Rumolumeni), (Saint John's campus along Abe road Port Harcourt), CASOR School of Nursing chapter, CASOR Federal College of Education, Technical, Omoku Campus and CASOR Ignatius Ajuru University of Education, Ndele Campus.

The Rivers State University (RSU) chapter has 22 executive council members headed by the President to service for one academic session. This chapter also has an Enlarged Executive Council referred to as ENEC. The ENEC is comprised of all the 22 EXCO members, out-gone EXCO members that are still pursuing their first degree in Rivers State University, Hall Pastors, Academic Advisers, CASORITES serving in affiliate bodies, for example, Joint Christian Campus Fellowship (JCCF), CASOR sub-zone to National levels. Very vital information about these persons and CASOR activities can be obtained from the fellowship semester program. This information includes: The names of the 22EXCO members, unit Heads/Coordinators, Committee/unit members, Hall Pastors, Patrons, Fellowship activities, etc., it is advisable that members of the fellowship should know the names and positions of their leaders
