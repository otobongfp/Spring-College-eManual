CASOR GENERAL INFORMATION

Christ Ambassadors Students Outreach (CASOR), Nigeria is a inter-denominational, Pentecostal, Evangelistic and Charismatic Fellowship of Students who unit to express the person and claims of Jesus Christ to their University, Polytechnics and Higher Colleges and to call others into relationship with Him.

OUR BELIEFS

• We believe the Bible to be the inspired and holy word of God (2 Tim. 3:16).

• We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons; God the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit (Matt. 28:l9).

• We believe also in the second coming of Christ Jesus (1 Thess. 4:15– 18).

• We believe in the redemption power of the blood of Christ through repentance (Luke 20:41-47).

• The regeneration of the Holy Spirit for personal salvation (Acts 1:8), and

• Baptism of the Holy Spirit with evidence of speaking in tongues.(Act 2:5).

MEMBERSHIP

Membership is open to all Christians and intending ones on campus irrespective of denominational and cultural background, ethnic group, social status and nationality.

ACTIVITIES OF THE FELLOWSHIP

The fellowship meets from time to time for the purpose of worship and for activities such as Evangelism, Missions, Bible Study, Prayer Meetings, Hostel and Classroom Devotions, Media Broadcasts, Rallies, Seminars, Symposia, Exposition/Teachings, Drama/Film shows, Music, Recreational Activities, Praise Fellowships. Visits, Inter/Intra Department Meetings and such others as may be considered as Family, Meetings and such others as may be considered adequate and appropriate to the growth of a Christian students.

FELLOWSHIP DAYS

Sunday, Wednesday and other day(s) that deemed necessary by the EXCO.

RELATIONSHIP WITH OTHER CHRISTIAN RELIGIOUS BODIES

The fellowship recognizes and is affiliated to this parent bodies CASOR at the Sub-zonal and National levels, the Joint Christian Campus Fellowship (JCCF) and the individual sister fellowship in JCCF and the thunders bolt. The fellowship docs not arrange or partake in any joint activity(ies) with any other religious body(ies) with contradictory doctrine.
