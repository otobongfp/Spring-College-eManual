SPRING COLLEGE GENERAL ORIENTATION

The Spring College is a unit under Christ Ambassador. Students' Outreach, Rivers State University Chapter. It is one of the units recognized as CASOR National Level. It goes by different names in different local chapters such as -Ambassadors Bible College, Foundation Bible Class, etc. no matter the name a chapter chooses to adopt, the aims and objectives remain the same. In RSU, the Spring College is provided for in Article 5 of CASOR Constitution.
History of the Spring College
The Spring College was established out of a desire expressed by the fresh students of the 1997/1998 academic session to have a forum for discussing issues of interest to their faith in Christ, their wellbeing as students on campus and as members of CASOR. These students organized themselves and held meetings together on their own. The Spring College was later officially inaugurated in the 1998/1999 session by the then President, Amb. Lesuanu Nwigoa.
Aims/Objectives
1. To provide a forum for beginners and / or new converts to good orientation of the Bible and the campus
2. To foster the spirit of love and unity amongst beginners and /or new converts
3. To ensure continued commitment of beginners and / or new converts to CHRIST and CASOR while in school
4. To give new members of the fellowship a proper orientation on the beliefs of the fellowship
5. To lay a strong foundation of faith for people that just gave their lives to Christ
6. To challenge new members unto higher height in faith
7. To find out problems of new members - spiritual, .academic, physical, social, etc. and communicate such to the EXCOs through the welfare committee for proper action.
8. To build strong Christians who can lead the fellowship and or other Christian body.
9. To challenge those not baptized in the Holy Ghost to see the need and help them actualize it.
10. To challenge new members to live the life of Ambassadors of Christ in the school and everywhere.
Members of the Spring College
The following categories of students are to attend the spring college:
1. Fresh students who were Christians before admission into the university
2. Old and fresh students who just gave their lives to Christ
3. Old students who have been Christians but just joining the fellowship
4. Old students (CASORITES) who have not gone through the Spring College training
5. There is no exemption on the ground of age, marital status, spiritual and social position. Note that there is an existing national order that anyone who does not pass through the college, should neither be allowed to vote nor stand for election into any office, should not be given any appointment or serve in any active capacity. The spring college is run/conducted by a Spring Provost with the help of an Assistant Spring Provost (the Bible Study Secretary) and supervised by the fellowship President. They are assisted by Spring Facilitators and an elected Spring College Executive Council.
